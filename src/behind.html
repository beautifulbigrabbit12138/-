-The Jurassic that took two years to prepare ▼

In terms of picture conception, Michael Clayton, who is also the original work and screenwriter, also began to participate in the creation of the storyboard, and worked with the art director Rick Carter to recreate the dinosaur world!

However, the original work was full of a large number of dinosaur species, and the first thing the crew had to deal with was to reduce 15 species to 6. After that, it is necessary to carry out crazy data collection to show the 100% true face of the dinosaurs. After the co-superviser Lata Ryan joined, the main force gathered the post-special effects force and was responsible for using the computer drawing technology that was new at that time to draw the dinosaurs and put them on the film.

Later, through meetings with different visual effects companies, director Spielberg found that the original use of the most traditional film real-life shooting, with amazing computer drawing technology, the effect was the most frerrening. Finally, the special effects of the film are called "Dream Group" in the industry. The members include the late Stan Winston, Phil Tippett and Dennis Mure from Industrial Light and Magic (ILM). N, and Michael Lantieri, who worked with Spielberg on Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade.

-Create a 1:1 mechanical Tyranosaurus Rex ▼

The 6-meter-high Tyrannosaurus Rex is made of glass fiber and nearly 1,500 kilograms of clay. The outer skin is elastic latex. The painter paints the skin with a large amount of pigments. The interior is mechanically operated by hydraulic technology for military purposes. The platform of Tyrannosaurus Rex and Tyrannosaurus Rex itself also have computer boards for remote control.

In addition, there are also other volumes of mechanical Tyrannosaurus Rex to match different scenes and shots for puppet shooting. One of the small ones called "Waldo" is responsible for the rehearsal, allowing the computer to record its movements, and then complete the final action with a one-to-one version with a height of 6 meters, just like shooting a live-action movie!

For Jurassic Park, Stan Winston Studio hired a total of 60 painters, mechanical engineers and puppeteers. In addition to Tyrannosaurus Rex, the mechanical models also include the 1.8-meter-high Athlon, the Long-necked Brachiosaurus, the sick Triceratops, the Chicken Crown Dragon, the Double Ridge Dragon and the Speed Dragon Baby, all of which play important roles in the movie!
