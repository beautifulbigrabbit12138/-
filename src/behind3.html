Truth and difference

Stephen Brusart, a professor at the University of Edinburgh, served as a paleontology consultant for this film. Every dinosaur that appeared in the movie once existed and was recognized by Brusart.

At the same time, the dinosaurs in the Jurassic series are also different from their real images, because scientists use the genes of modern animals (such as frogs) to supplement the missing fragments of dinosaur genes.

In the film, the only dinosaur species with a fixed shape are Tyrannosaurus rex and Double Crown rex, because they have appeared in the previous series.

As for other types of dinosaurs, the design team can let go to create images that are different from the previous ones, because dinosaur scholars have made many breakthroughs in recent years.

About Double Crown Dragon

Double-crowned dragon is the only dinosaur that is completely filmed on the screen, and its picture does not superimpose any visual effects or use visual replacement.

This film is also the first Jurassic series to use physical dinosaurs. The double-crowned dragon is controlled by 11 to 12 staff members.

In order to reproduce the attack scene of the double-crowned dragon spitting mucus in Jurassic Park, the technicians mixed super mucus, cakes, food coloring, water and a thickener called methyl cellulose, which is often used as a fast food milkshake.
